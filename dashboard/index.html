🤖 Model Explanation

Understanding the machine learning approach behind the predictions.

🎯

Target Variable

Dengue risk level classified as Low, Medium, or High. High risk is defined as weekly cases ≥ 75th percentile of historical baseline for that district.

📐

Input Features

District identifier (encoded), weekly case history, mean/max/min temperature, precipitation sum, sunshine duration, daylight duration, wind speed, and evapotranspiration index.

🔧

Feature Engineering

Lag features (2–4 weeks) for temperature and rainfall to capture mosquito breeding cycle delays. Daylight-to-sunshine ratios and district-specific rolling averages.

📏

Why Scaling?

StandardScaler (Z-score) normalizes all numerical features to mean=0, std=1. This prevents large-magnitude features (e.g. rainfall in mm) from dominating distance-based algorithms and PCA.

🧬

PCA Reduction

Principal Component Analysis extracts 7 principal components that capture >90% of variance from the weather feature set, resolving multicollinearity and reducing overfitting risk.

🏷️

District Encoding

Target encoding maps each district to its mean historical risk score, converting categorical district names to continuous numerical values without high-cardinality one-hot explosion.

🛡️ Prevention Recommendations

Recommendations update based on your simulator prediction. Run the simulator first for personalized advice.

Current Risk Level: Not simulated
✅

Low Risk — Maintain Vigilance

Risk is currently low. Continue routine practices to keep it that way.

🧹

Regular Cleaning

Continue regular cleaning of surroundings. Remove fallen leaves and debris that can trap water.

🪣

Monitor Water Containers

Check water storage containers weekly. Ensure they are tightly covered to prevent mosquito breeding.

📋

Stay Informed

Monitor local health alerts. Track weekly case counts in your district for early warning.

⚠️

Medium Risk — Take Preventive Action

Elevated risk detected. Take targeted precautions to minimize mosquito breeding opportunities.

💧

Remove Stagnant Water

Eliminate all sources of standing water — flower pots, tyres, gutters, and roof drainage channels.

🌿

Inspect Gardens & Drains

Check gardens, drains, and low-lying areas after rainfall. Clear blockages that cause water pooling.

🦟

Use Mosquito Protection

Apply mosquito repellent, use mosquito nets, and wear long-sleeved clothing especially during dawn and dusk.

🏘️

Community Awareness

Notify neighbors and participate in community clean-up activities to reduce collective breeding sites.

🚨

High Risk — Urgent Action Required

Critical dengue risk level. Immediate and coordinated action is essential.

📢

Increase Community Awareness

Coordinate with local health authorities and community leaders to escalate awareness campaigns immediately.

🔍

Inspect All Breeding Places

Conduct systematic inspection of all potential breeding sites. Report large breeding grounds to local health officers.

🏥

Seek Medical Help Promptly

If dengue symptoms appear (high fever, severe headache, rash, joint pain), seek medical attention immediately without delay.

🏛️

Follow Public Health Guidelines

Follow all instructions from the Ministry of Health and National Dengue Control Unit. Cooperate with fogging operations.
